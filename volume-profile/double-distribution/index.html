Double Distribution: Multiple Fair Value Zones

Master Double Distribution analysis to identify two distinct fair value zones within a profile. Learn to trade between distributions, spot accumulation patterns, and identify potential breakouts or breakdowns.

Double Distribution Quick Guide

Double Distribution shows two distinct fair value zones separated by a low volume gap. This pattern reveals accumulation in one zone and distribution in another, with the gap serving as a key breakout area.

Double Distribution Formula

Distribution 1 = Value Area 1 (Lower Zone)
Distribution 2 = Value Area 2 (Upper Zone)
Gap = Low Volume Area Between Distributions
Gap Width = Distribution 2 Low - Distribution 1 High
Gap Strength = Volume in Gap / Average Volume
Distribution Ratio = Volume Zone 1 / Volume Zone 2
Pattern Bias = Higher Volume Zone (Accumulation or Distribution)

How Double Distribution Works

Double Distribution works by identifying two separate fair value zones within a single profile. These zones develop when trading occurs at two distinct price areas, often reflecting different market phases. The gap between distributions represents a price area with little acceptance, making it a key breakout zone.

Double Distribution Strategies

Gap Breakout Strategy

Trade breakouts through the double distribution gap with momentum and volume confirmation

Distribution Rotation Strategy

Trade between distributions, buying at lower zone support and selling at upper zone resistance

Volume Shift Strategy

Follow volume accumulation shifting between distributions for directional bias

Advanced Distribution Detection

def detect_double_distribution(price_levels, volumes, min_distribution_size=5):
    # Find potential distribution centers (POCs)
    pocs = []
    for i in range(len(volumes)):
        if volumes[i] > volumes[i-1] and volumes[i] > volumes[i+1]:
            pocs.append(i)
    
    # Group into distributions
    distributions = []
    current_dist = [pocs[0]]
    for i in range(1, len(pocs)):
        if pocs[i] - pocs[i-1] > min_distribution_size:
            distributions.append(current_dist)
            current_dist = [pocs[i]]
        else:
            current_dist.append(pocs[i])
    distributions.append(current_dist)
    
    return distributions

Distribution Implementation Tips

Use multiple timeframe analysis to confirm distribution patterns. Monitor volume at gap boundaries for breakout confirmation. Combine double distribution with other patterns for stronger signals. Adjust minimum distribution size based on market volatility.

Frequently Asked Questions

What causes Double Distribution patterns?

Double Distribution patterns typically occur when trading shifts between two different price areas. This often happens during news events, market openings, or when institutional traders move positions between accumulation and distribution zones.

How reliable is Double Distribution for trading?

Double Distribution patterns are 70-80% reliable for identifying key support/resistance and breakout levels. The reliability increases when confirmed with volume analysis and multiple timeframe studies.

Can Double Distribution occur in all markets?

Yes, Double Distribution patterns occur in all liquid markets but are most common in futures and equities. They're less frequent in forex due to continuous trading but can appear during major sessions.

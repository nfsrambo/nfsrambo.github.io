Passive Volume: Limit Order Liquidity Analysis

Understand passive volume from limit orders providing market liquidity. Learn to identify institutional support/resistance zones and spot where passive orders create key trading levels.

Passive Volume Quick Guide

Passive volume shows limit order liquidity at price levels. High passive volume creates strong support/resistance as institutions place orders waiting for price to reach their levels.

Passive Volume Calculation

Limit Orders at Bid = Passive Sellers
Limit Orders at Ask = Passive Buyers
Passive Volume = Σ(Limit Order Volume at Price)
Passive Ratio = Passive Volume / Total Volume
Liquidity Score = Passive Volume at Level / Average Passive Volume

How Passive Volume Works

Passive volume represents limit orders that provide liquidity to the market. These resting orders show where traders are willing to buy or sell without rushing. High passive volume at price levels creates natural support and resistance that often holds during price tests.

Passive Volume Strategies

Passive Support Strategy

Buy when price approaches high passive bid volume with reversal confirmation

Passive Resistance Strategy

Short when price approaches high passive ask volume with rejection confirmation

Passive Build Strategy

Watch for passive volume building at levels for potential breakout setups

Frequently Asked Questions

What is the difference between passive and aggressive volume?

Passive volume comes from limit orders that provide liquidity. Aggressive volume comes from market orders that take liquidity. Passive shows where traders are willing to wait, aggressive shows urgency.

How reliable are passive volume levels for trading?

Passive volume levels are 70-75% reliable as support/resistance. The reliability increases with multiple level tests and when combined with price action confirmation.

Can passive volume be detected in all markets?

Passive volume detection requires level 2 data or order book information. It's most accurate in futures and equities markets where order book data is available.

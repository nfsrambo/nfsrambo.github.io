CCI - Commodity Channel Index: Formula & Signals

Learn CCI formula, overbought/oversold levels, divergence patterns, trend identification, and get code in Python, Rust, Go, and 19 more languages.

Quick Answer

CCI compares current price to its statistical average. Think of it as a volatility-adjusted momentum indicator. Above +100 = price is stretched high (overbought). Below -100 = price is stretched low (oversold). CCI helps spot trend strength and potential reversals.

Formula

Typical Price = (High + Low + Close) / 3
Mean Deviation = Average |Typical Price - SMA(Typical Price, N)|
CCI = (Typical Price - SMA(Typical Price, N)) / (0.015 × Mean Deviation)
Where N = 20 periods (standard)

CCI Signal Interpretation

CCI > +100

Overbought - price may be too high. Potential sell or pullback ahead.

CCI < -100

Oversold - price may be too low. Potential buy or bounce ahead.

CCI crosses above +100

Strong bullish momentum starting. Trend could continue higher.

CCI crosses below -100

Strong bearish momentum starting. Trend could continue lower.

Divergence with price

Price and CCI moving opposite directions. Reversal likely ahead.

How CCI Works

CCI measures the difference between current price and its statistical average, normalized by the mean deviation. This makes it a volatility-adjusted indicator - readings are more meaningful during volatile periods. CCI above +100 indicates price is significantly above average (overbought). Below -100 indicates price is significantly below average (oversold).

Step-by-Step CCI Calculation

1

Calculate Typical Price: (High + Low + Close) / 3

2

Calculate SMA of Typical Price over N periods (typically 20)

3

Calculate Mean Deviation: average absolute difference between Typical Price and SMA

4

Calculate CCI: (Typical Price - SMA) / (0.015 × Mean Deviation)

5

Read above +100 as overbought, below -100 as oversold

6

Watch for divergence between price and CCI for reversals

CCI Trading Strategies

Overbought/Oversold Strategy

Buy when CCI crosses above -100 (leaving oversold). Sell when CCI crosses below +100 (leaving overbought). Works best in ranging markets.

beginner

CCI Divergence Strategy

Look for bullish divergence (price lower low, CCI higher low) - buy. Look for bearish divergence (price higher high, CCI lower high) - sell. High probability reversal signals.

intermediate

CCI Trend Following Strategy

In strong trends, buy when CCI crosses above +100 (momentum confirmation). Sell when CCI crosses below -100 (momentum confirmation). Trend continues while CCI stays in extreme territory.

intermediate

CCI + ADX Strategy

Use ADX to confirm trend strength. Take CCI signals when ADX > 25. Avoid CCI signals in sideways markets (ADX < 20).

advanced

Pros & Cons

Advantages

  • -Volatility-adjusted - works across different market conditions
  • -Clear overbought/oversold levels (+100/-100)
  • -Divergence signals are highly reliable
  • -Works on all timeframes and markets
  • -Simple to implement in code

Limitations

  • -Can stay overbought/oversold in strong trends
  • -Lagging indicator - uses historical data
  • -False signals in sideways markets
  • -Period optimization can overfit
  • -Less effective in low volatility environments

History

CCI was developed by Donald Lambert in 1980. Lambert created it for commodities trading but it has since been applied to all financial markets. The 20-period standard and +100/-100 levels were derived from extensive testing on commodity futures data.

Best Market Conditions

CCI works best in trending markets where momentum is clear. In sideways markets, it gives false signals. Use ADX to confirm trend strength - take CCI signals when ADX > 25. In low volatility markets, CCI readings may not reach extreme levels.

Common Mistakes

  • -Trading every overbought/oversold signal without trend confirmation
  • -Using CCI alone without other indicators
  • -Ignoring divergence signals - high probability setups
  • -Using default 20-period for all timeframes without testing
  • -Expecting CCI to predict exact tops and bottoms

Frequently Asked Questions

What is the best CCI period for crypto trading?

For crypto, 14-period CCI works well for day trading. 20-period is standard for swing trading. For Bitcoin, 14-period works best. For altcoins with higher volatility, 10-period provides faster signals.

How to use CCI with Bollinger Bands?

Use Bollinger Bands for price context and CCI for momentum. Buy when price at lower band and CCI shows oversold. Sell when price at upper band and CCI shows overbought. Stronger signals when both align.

What does the 0.015 constant in CCI formula mean?

The 0.015 constant scales CCI readings so approximately 70-80% of values fall between -100 and +100. This makes the indicator consistent across different markets and timeframes.

Williams %R - Momentum Oscillator: Formula & Strategy

Learn Williams %R formula, overbought/oversold signals, divergence patterns, momentum measurement, and get code in Python, Rust, Go, and 19 more languages.

Simulator not available for this indicator

Quick Answer

Williams %R measures momentum on a -100 to 0 scale. Think of it as Stochastic's inverted cousin. Below -80 = oversold (buy zone). Above -20 = overbought (sell zone). It shows where price is in its recent range, similar to Stochastic but with inverted readings.

Formula

Williams %R = ((Highest High - Close) / (Highest High - Lowest Low)) × -100
Where:
Highest High = Highest high over N periods
Lowest Low = Lowest low over N periods
N = 14 periods (standard)
Range: 0 to -100

Williams %R Signal Interpretation

%R above -20 (Overbought)

Overbought - price near top of range. Potential sell signal.

%R below -80 (Oversold)

Oversold - price near bottom of range. Potential buy signal.

%R crossing above -80

Bullish - leaving oversold. Momentum turning up. Potential buy.

%R crossing below -20

Bearish - leaving overbought. Momentum turning down. Potential sell.

Divergence with price

Price and %R moving opposite. Reversal likely.

How Williams %R Works

Williams %R measures where the closing price falls within the high-low range over a period. When close is near the high, %R is near 0 (overbought). When close is near the low, %R is near -100 (oversold). The negative scale is inverted compared to Stochastic, but the interpretation is similar.

Step-by-Step Williams %R Calculation

Find highest high over N periods

2

Find lowest low over N periods

3

Calculate: (Highest High - Close) / (Highest High - Lowest Low)

4

Multiply by -100

5

Values above -20 = overbought

6

Values below -80 = oversold

Williams %R Trading Strategies

Overbought/Oversold Strategy

Buy when %R crosses above -80 (leaving oversold). Sell when %R crosses below -20 (leaving overbought). Simple reversal strategy.

beginner

Divergence Strategy

Bullish divergence: price lower low, %R higher low. Bearish divergence: price higher high, %R lower high. Very reliable signals.

Williams %R + Trend Strategy

Pros & Cons

Advantages

  • -Clear overbought/oversold levels
  • -Divergence signals are reliable
  • -Works across all markets
  • -Similar to Stochastic but simpler

Limitations

  • -Less widely known than RSI
  • -Can stay extreme in strong trends
  • -False signals in choppy markets
  • -Period selection affects performance
  • -Best used with other indicators

History

Williams %R was developed by Larry Williams, a famous commodities trader, in the 1970s. It was designed as a faster, more responsive version of the stochastic oscillator. The negative scale (-100 to 0) is unique to this indicator.

Best Market Conditions

Williams %R works best in trending markets where momentum is clear. In sideways markets, it can give false signals. Use with ADX for best results.

Common Mistakes

  • -Using Williams %R alone without confirmation
  • -Trading every extreme without context
  • -Ignoring divergence signals
  • -Using wrong period for timeframe
  • -Using in sideways markets

Frequently Asked Questions

What is the best Williams %R setting for crypto?

For crypto, 14-period %R works for swing trading. For day trading, use 7-10 periods. Bitcoin works with 14-period. Altcoins may need 10-period.

How to use Williams %R with MACD?

Use %R for momentum and MACD for trend. When both bullish, trend is strong. %R divergence with MACD = reversal signal.

What is the difference between Williams %R and Stochastic?

Williams %R uses a -100 to 0 scale (inverted). Stochastic uses 0 to 100 scale. Both measure the same concept. %R is simpler with no smoothing.

Fibonacci Retracement - Levels: Formula & Strategy

Learn Fibonacci Retracement levels, support/resistance identification, pullback trading, and get code in Python, Rust, Go, and 19 more languages.

Quick Answer

Fibonacci Retracement identifies where price might pause or reverse in a trend. Think of it as a roadmap of support and resistance during pullbacks. 0.618 (Golden Ratio), 0.500, and 0.382 are the key levels. Price often bounces from these levels to continue the trend.

Formula

Retracement Level = Start + (End - Start) × (1 - Level)
Where:
Start = Swing Low (for uptrend)
End = Swing High (for uptrend)
Level = Fibonacci level (0.236, 0.382, 0.500, 0.618, 0.786)

Fibonacci Retracement Signal Interpretation

Price bounces from 0.618 level

Strong support/resistance. 0.618 is the most important level.

Price bounces from 0.382 level

Shallow pullback - strong trend. Trend continuation likely.

Price breaks below 0.786

Deep retracement - trend may be weakening. Potential reversal.

Multiple levels align

Confluence area - stronger support/resistance. Higher probability trade.

Price between 0.618 and 0.786

Deep retracement zone. Watch for reversal or breakout.

How Fibonacci Retracement Works

Fibonacci Retracement uses mathematical ratios derived from the Fibonacci sequence. When applied to a price swing, these levels show potential areas where price might pull back before continuing in the trend direction. The 0.618 level is the Golden Ratio - the most important Fibonacci level. These levels act as dynamic support and resistance during trend pullbacks.

Step-by-Step Fibonacci Retracement Calculation

1

Identify a swing high and swing low

2

Calculate the difference (distance) between them

3

Apply Fibonacci levels: 0.236, 0.382, 0.500, 0.618, 0.786

4

For uptrend: High - (High - Low) × Level

5

For downtrend: Low + (High - Low) × Level

6

Plot levels as horizontal lines on chart

Fibonacci Retracement Trading Strategies

Golden Ratio Strategy

Trade at 0.618 retracement level - the most watched level. Buy at 0.618 in uptrends, sell at 0.618 in downtrends. Use with trend confirmation.

beginner

Confluence Strategy

Look for Fibonacci levels aligning with moving averages or previous support/resistance. Multiple confluences = stronger signals.

intermediate

Fibonacci + RSI Strategy

Buy when price retraces to 0.618 and RSI shows oversold in uptrend. Sell when price retraces to 0.618 and RSI shows overbought in downtrend.

intermediate

Fibonacci + Candlestick Strategy

Look for reversal candlestick patterns at Fibonacci levels. Pin bars, engulfing patterns, and dojis at Fibonacci levels are high probability setups.

advanced

Pros & Cons

Advantages

  • -Objective support/resistance levels
  • -Works across all markets and timeframes
  • -0.618 is a reliable reversal level
  • -Easy to identify and use
  • -Combines well with other indicators

Limitations

  • -Subjective swing point selection
  • -Multiple levels can be confusing
  • -Price can ignore Fibonacci levels
  • -Not predictive - levels are estimates
  • -Works best with trend confirmation

History

Fibonacci ratios were derived from the Fibonacci sequence discovered by Leonardo Fibonacci in the 13th century. The ratios were applied to financial markets by traders in the 20th century. R.N. Elliott used Fibonacci ratios in his Wave Theory. Today, Fibonacci retracement is a standard tool in technical analysis.

Best Market Conditions

Fibonacci retracement works best in trending markets where price is making clear swings. In sideways markets, Fibonacci levels may not provide clear signals. Use with ADX to confirm trend strength.

Common Mistakes

  • -Using Fibonacci alone without trend confirmation
  • -Drawing from wrong swing points
  • -Expecting price to stop exactly at Fibonacci levels
  • -Ignoring confluence with other levels
  • -Using Fibonacci in sideways markets

Frequently Asked Questions

What is the best Fibonacci setting for crypto?

Standard levels (0.236, 0.382, 0.500, 0.618, 0.786) work for all markets. For crypto, 0.618 and 0.786 are often more reliable due to higher volatility. Add 0.886 for deeper retracements.

How to use Fibonacci with MACD?

Use Fibonacci for price levels and MACD for momentum confirmation. When price at 0.618 and MACD shows bullish divergence in uptrend, it's a strong buy signal.

What is the difference between retracement and extension?

Retracement measures pullbacks within a trend (0-100% of a swing). Extension measures price targets beyond the swing (100%+). Retracement for entries, extensions for targets.

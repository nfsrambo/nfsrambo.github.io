OBV - On Balance Volume: Formula & Signals

Learn OBV formula, volume flow analysis, divergence patterns, trend confirmation, and get code in Python, Rust, Go, and 19 more languages.

Quick Answer

OBV tracks cumulative volume flow to see who's winning - buyers or sellers. Rising OBV = buyers in control (bullish). Falling OBV = sellers in control (bearish). When OBV diverges from price, it's telling you the trend may be about to reverse.

Formula

If Close > Previous Close: OBV = Previous OBV + Volume
If Close < Previous Close: OBV = Previous OBV - Volume
If Close = Previous Close: OBV = Previous OBV
Cumulative calculation from start of data

OBV Signal Interpretation

OBV rising

Buying pressure increasing - buyers are in control. Bullish.

OBV falling

Selling pressure increasing - sellers are in control. Bearish.

OBV making higher highs

Strong buying interest - price likely to follow higher.

OBV making lower lows

Strong selling interest - price likely to follow lower.

Divergence with price

Price and OBV moving opposite directions. Trend reversal likely.

How OBV Works

OBV calculates cumulative volume flow by adding volume on up days and subtracting volume on down days. The result shows whether volume is flowing into or out of a stock. Rising OBV indicates accumulation (buying pressure). Falling OBV indicates distribution (selling pressure). OBV often leads price, making it a leading indicator for trend changes.

Step-by-Step OBV Calculation

1

Compare current close to previous close

2

If close is higher, add volume to OBV

3

If close is lower, subtract volume from OBV

4

If close is unchanged, OBV stays same

5

Plot OBV alongside price for comparison

6

Watch for divergence between OBV and price

OBV Trading Strategies

OBV Trend Confirmation

Price rising with OBV rising = strong uptrend. Price rising with OBV falling = weak uptrend. Price falling with OBV falling = strong downtrend.

beginner

OBV Divergence Strategy

Buy on bullish divergence (price lower low, OBV higher low). Sell on bearish divergence (price higher high, OBV lower high). One of the most reliable reversal signals.

intermediate

OBV Breakout Strategy

Price breakout with OBV confirming higher high = strong breakout. Price breakout with OBV making lower high = weak breakout. Use for entry/exit timing.

intermediate

OBV + Volume Spike Strategy

OBV rising with volume spike = strong accumulation. OBV falling with volume spike = strong distribution. Volume spikes confirm OBV direction.

advanced

Pros & Cons

Advantages

  • -Simple to calculate and understand
  • -Shows actual buying and selling pressure
  • -Divergence signals are highly reliable
  • -Often leads price - early warning system
  • -Works across all markets

Limitations

  • -Cumulative can give false trends over long periods
  • -Doesn't have overbought/oversold levels
  • -Less effective without volume confirmation
  • -Can show divergence multiple times before reversal

History

OBV was developed by Joseph Granville in his 1963 book 'Granville's New Key to Stock Market Profits'. Granville, a well-known market analyst, recognized that volume precedes price. OBV was one of the first indicators to incorporate volume into technical analysis.

Best Market Conditions

OBV works best in liquid markets with high volume. It's particularly effective in stocks and crypto. In low volume markets, OBV signals are less reliable. Use with ADX to confirm trend strength.

Common Mistakes

  • -Using OBV alone without price confirmation
  • -Ignoring divergence signals - the most reliable OBV signal
  • -Using OBV on low volume markets
  • -Expecting OBV to give exact price targets
  • -Using OBV without smoothing in noisy markets

Frequently Asked Questions

How to use OBV with MACD?

Use MACD for momentum and OBV for volume confirmation. When both confirm trend direction, signals are stronger. OBV divergence with MACD momentum changes gives high probability reversal signals.

What is the best smoothing period for OBV?

A 20-period SMA of OBV is commonly used for smoothing. Day traders use 10-period for faster signals. Swing traders use 20-50 period for smoother trends.

Does OBV work in crypto markets?

Yes, OBV works well in crypto markets where volume data is reliable. OBV divergence is particularly effective for catching Bitcoin trend reversals on daily and 4-hour timeframes.

Quick Answer

Ichimoku Cloud is like a complete trading dashboard. Price above the cloud = uptrend. Price below = downtrend. Inside cloud = sideways. Tenkan crossing above Kijun = bullish. Cloud thickness tells you support/resistance strength.

Formula

Tenkan-sen = (Highest High + Lowest Low) / 2 over 9 periods
Kijun-sen = (Highest High + Lowest Low) / 2 over 26 periods
Senkou Span A = (Tenkan-sen + Kijun-sen) / 2 (projected 26 periods)
Senkou Span B = (Highest High + Lowest Low) / 2 over 52 periods (projected 26 periods)
Chikou Span = Close plotted 26 periods back
Cloud = Area between Span A and Span B

Ichimoku Signal Interpretation

Price above Cloud

Bullish trend - strong uptrend. Look for pullbacks to Kijun for entries.

Price below Cloud

Bearish trend - strong downtrend. Look for rallies to Kijun for shorts.

Inside Cloud

Sideways market - no trend. Avoid trend strategies, use mean-reversion.

Tenkan crosses above Kijun

Bullish signal - momentum turning up. Potential buy entry.

Tenkan crosses below Kijun

Bearish signal - momentum turning down. Potential sell entry.

Ichimoku Cloud is a complete trading system, not just an indicator. It provides support/resistance (cloud), momentum (Tenkan/Kijun), and trend direction (price vs cloud) all in one view. The cloud is projected 26 periods forward, giving predictive support/resistance levels. This forward-looking feature sets Ichimoku apart from lagging indicators.

Step-by-Step Ichimoku Calculation

1

Calculate Tenkan-sen: (9-period high + 9-period low) / 2

2

Calculate Kijun-sen: (26-period high + 26-period low) / 2

3

Calculate Senkou Span A: (Tenkan + Kijun) / 2, projected 26 periods ahead

4

Calculate Senkou Span B: (52-period high + 52-period low) / 2, projected 26 periods ahead

5

The cloud is the area between Span A and Span B

6

Plot Chikou Span: Current close shifted 26 periods back

Ichimoku Trading Strategies

Cloud Breakout Strategy

Buy when price breaks above the cloud with conviction. Sell when price breaks below. Cloud breaks often lead to sustained moves.

beginner

Tenkan/Kijun Crossover

Buy when Tenkan crosses above Kijun, with price above cloud. Sell when Tenkan crosses below Kijun, with price below cloud. Classic entry signal.

intermediate

Kijun Bounce Strategy

In uptrends (price above cloud), buy when price pulls back to Kijun and bounces. In downtrends, sell when price rallies to Kijun and fails.

intermediate

Cloud Thickness Strategy

Thick cloud = strong support/resistance. Thin cloud = weak support/resistance. Use thickness to gauge breakout probability - thin cloud breaks easier.

advanced

Pros & Cons

Advantages

  • -Complete system - trend, momentum, support, resistance all in one
  • -Forward-looking - cloud projects support/resistance ahead
  • -Works on all timeframes - from minutes to months
  • -Clear visual signals - easy to read at a glance
  • -Multiple confirmation signals - reduces false entries

Limitations

  • -Complex to learn - many components to understand
  • -Lagging in strong trends - signals come after move starts
  • -Less effective in sideways markets - cloud gives false signals
  • -Default settings may need adjustment for different assets
  • -Can look cluttered on short timeframes

History

Ichimoku Kinko Hyo was developed by Goichi Hosoda, a Japanese journalist, in the 1930s. He spent decades refining the system before publishing it. 'Ichimoku' means 'one look' in Japanese - reflecting that traders can gauge market conditions at a glance. Hosoda's system remains one of the most comprehensive technical analysis tools.

Best Market Conditions

Ichimoku works best in trending markets where cloud breaks lead to sustained moves. It's less effective in ranging markets where price oscillates inside the cloud. Use ADX to confirm trend strength - trade Ichimoku signals when ADX > 25.

Common Mistakes

  • -Overcomplicating - focus on price vs cloud first, then crossovers
  • -Ignoring cloud thickness - thick cloud is strong support/resistance
  • -Using on short timeframes without adjusting settings
  • -Trading against the cloud - the cloud is powerful support/resistance
  • -Entering without waiting for confirmation - wait for price to break cloud or crossover

Frequently Asked Questions

What are the best Ichimoku settings for crypto?

Standard (9,26,52) works for most cryptos. For Bitcoin, consider (10,30,60) to reduce false signals. For altcoins with higher volatility, (7,22,44) provides faster signals. Always backtest on your specific asset.

How to use Ichimoku with MACD?

Use MACD for momentum confirmation. When Ichimoku shows bullish (price above cloud) and MACD confirms bullish momentum, entries are stronger. Divergence between the two can signal potential reversals.

What does Chikou Span indicate?

Chikou Span shows current price projected 26 periods back. When Chikou is above price from 26 periods ago, bullish. Below, bearish. Crossovers between Chikou and historical price give additional signals.

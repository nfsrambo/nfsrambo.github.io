Bollinger Bands - Volatility Bands: Formula & Strategy

Quick Answer

Bollinger Bands wrap around price with upper and lower bands based on volatility. When price hits the upper band, it's stretched - potential reversal down. Hit the lower band? Stretched down - potential bounce up. The squeeze between bands often signals a big move coming.

Formula

Middle Band = SMA(Close, N)
Upper Band = Middle Band + (K × Standard Deviation)
Lower Band = Middle Band - (K × Standard Deviation)
Standard N = 20, K = 2.0
Where SMA = Simple Moving Average

Bollinger Bands Signal Interpretation

Price touches upper band

Overbought condition - potential sell or pullback. Price may be extended.

Price touches lower band

Oversold condition - potential buy or bounce. Price may be oversold.

Bands squeeze (narrow)

Volatility contraction - big move likely. Prepare for breakout.

Bands expand (widen)

Volatility expansion - trend in motion. Ride the momentum.

Price walks the band

Strong trend - price hugging the band. Trend continuation likely.

How Bollinger Bands Work

Bollinger Bands consist of a middle SMA line with upper and lower bands calculated using standard deviation. As volatility increases, bands widen. As volatility decreases, bands narrow. Price tends to stay within the bands 85-90% of the time, making band touches significant events. The squeeze pattern is particularly powerful - when bands narrow to their lowest point in months, volatility expansion is imminent.

Step-by-Step Bollinger Bands Calculation

1

Calculate SMA of closing prices over N periods (typically 20)

2

Calculate standard deviation of closing prices over same N periods

3

Upper band = SMA + (K × Standard Deviation)

4

Lower band = SMA - (K × Standard Deviation)

5

Monitor band width for squeeze patterns

6

Track price position relative to bands for signals

Bollinger Bands Trading Strategies

Band Reversal Strategy

Buy when price touches lower band and RSI shows oversold. Sell when price touches upper band and RSI shows overbought. Best in ranging markets.

beginner

Squeeze Breakout Strategy

When bands narrow to multi-month low, wait for price to break above upper or below lower band with volume. Enter in breakout direction. This catches explosive moves.

intermediate

Band Walk Strategy

In strong trends, price rides the band. Buy when price walks the upper band in uptrend. Sell when price walks lower band in downtrend. Trend continues as long as band hug continues.

intermediate

Double Bottom/Squeeze Combo

Look for double bottom at lower band followed by squeeze. When price breaks above middle band with increasing volume, enter long. High probability setup.

advanced

Pros & Cons

Advantages

  • -Adapts to volatility - bands widen in volatile markets, narrow in quiet markets
  • -Clear visual signals - easy to spot overbought/oversold conditions
  • -Squeeze pattern predicts big moves before they happen
  • -Works with other indicators - RSI, volume, and MACD complement well
  • -Applicable to all markets - stocks, forex, crypto, commodities

Limitations

  • -False signals in strong trends - price can hug the band for extended periods
  • -Lagging indicator - bands are based on historical volatility
  • -Squeeze doesn't tell direction - breakout can be up or down
  • -Period optimization can overfit - what works for one market may fail for another

History

Bollinger Bands were developed by John Bollinger in the 1980s. He introduced them in his 2002 book 'Bollinger on Bollinger Bands'. Bollinger, a Chartered Financial Analyst, created the bands to adapt to changing market conditions - a significant improvement over fixed-percentage bands. Today, they're among the most widely used volatility indicators.

Best Market Conditions

Bollinger Bands work in all market conditions but excel in ranging markets where price bounces between bands. In strong trends, price can walk the band - use ATR or ADX to confirm trend strength. The squeeze pattern is valuable in both trending and ranging markets as it predicts volatility expansion.

Common Mistakes

  • -Trading every band touch without confirmation - combine with RSI or volume
  • -Using default 20/2 setting for all markets - adjust based on volatility
  • -Ignoring the squeeze pattern - it's one of the most reliable signals
  • -Trading against the trend when bands are wide - strong trends can hug bands for weeks
  • -Using bands alone for direction - they show volatility, not momentum

Frequently Asked Questions

What is the best Bollinger Bands period for crypto trading?

For crypto, 20-period with 2 standard deviations works well. For Bitcoin, consider 20/2.5 to reduce false signals. For altcoins with higher volatility, 20/2.2 works better. Always backtest on your specific asset.

How to use Bollinger Bands with RSI?

Use RSI to confirm band touches - RSI above 70 at upper band confirms overbought. RSI below 30 at lower band confirms oversold. This combination reduces false signals by 30-40%.

What does Bollinger Bands squeeze indicate?

A squeeze indicates volatility contraction - the calm before the storm. When bands narrow to their tightest in months, expect a significant price move. The breakout direction is unknown - wait for price to break above or below bands with volume.

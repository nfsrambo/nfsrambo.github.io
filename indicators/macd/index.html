MACD - Moving Average Convergence Divergence

Learn MACD formula, histogram signals, crossover patterns, divergence detection, and get code in 22 programming languages.

Loading simulator...

Quick Answer

Think of MACD as a trend and momentum tracker in one. When the MACD line crosses above the signal line, momentum is shifting bullish - a potential buy signal. Cross below? Bearish momentum - potential sell. The histogram shows how strong the momentum really is.

Formula

MACD Line = 12-period EMA - 26-period EMA
Signal Line = 9-period EMA of MACD Line
Histogram = MACD Line - Signal Line
Where EMA = Exponential Moving Average

MACD Signal Interpretation

MACD crosses above Signal Line

Bullish crossover - momentum shifting up. Confirmed by rising histogram.

MACD crosses below Signal Line

Bearish crossover - momentum shifting down. Confirmed by falling histogram.

MACD above zero line

Bullish trend confirmed - price above its 26-period average. Look for pullbacks to buy.

MACD below zero line

Bearish trend confirmed - price below its 26-period average. Look for rallies to sell.

Histogram turns from negative to positive

Momentum flipping from bearish to bullish - early entry signal.

How MACD Works

MACD measures the relationship between two exponential moving averages - a fast one (12-period) and a slow one (26-period). When the fast MA pulls away from the slow MA, momentum builds. When they converge, momentum fades. The signal line (9-period EMA of MACD) acts as a trigger - crossovers tell you when momentum is shifting direction.

Step-by-Step MACD Calculation

1

Calculate 12-period EMA of closing prices

2

Calculate 26-period EMA of closing prices

3

Subtract 26 EMA from 12 EMA to get MACD line

4

Calculate 9-period EMA of MACD line to get signal line

5

Subtract signal line from MACD line to get histogram

6

Positive histogram = MACD above signal line (bullish)

7

Negative histogram = MACD below signal line (bearish)

MACD Trading Strategies

MACD Crossover Strategy

Buy when MACD line crosses above signal line. Sell when MACD line crosses below signal line. Best in trending markets - use ADX > 25 to filter out false signals in choppy conditions.

beginner

MACD Divergence Strategy

Bullish divergence: price makes lower low, MACD makes higher low → buy. Bearish divergence: price makes higher high, MACD makes lower high → sell. Most reliable on daily charts.

intermediate

MACD Zero Line Rejection

In uptrends, buy when MACD bounces off zero line from below. In downtrends, sell when MACD bounces off zero line from above. Works as a trend continuation signal.

intermediate

MACD Histogram Reversal

Buy when histogram turns from negative to positive. Sell when histogram turns from positive to negative. The histogram turning point often precedes the MACD line crossover by 1-2 bars.

advanced

Pros & Cons

Advantages

  • -Shows both trend direction and momentum in one indicator - two signals for the price of one
  • -Crossover signals are clear and actionable - no ambiguity about entry points
  • -Divergence signals are highly reliable - often catching major reversals early
  • -Works across all timeframes and markets - from 1-minute scalping to monthly position trading
  • -Zero line acts as an objective trend filter - above is bullish, below is bearish

Limitations

  • -Lagging indicator by nature - signals often appear after the move has started
  • -False signals in ranging/sideways markets - crossovers whip back and forth
  • -Crossover signals can be late in fast-moving markets - catch the middle of the move, not the start
  • -Multiple signals in choppy conditions - requires patience and discipline
  • -Standard settings may need adjustment - what works for stocks may not work for crypto

History

MACD was developed by Gerald Appel in the late 1970s and first published in his book 'The Moving Average Convergence Divergence Trading Method'. Appel discovered that by plotting the difference between two moving averages, he could identify momentum shifts earlier than traditional moving average crossovers. The standard (12,26,9) settings were chosen after analyzing decades of stock market data and remain the industry standard today.

Best Market Conditions

MACD performs best in trending markets where sustained directional moves occur. It's particularly effective in stocks with clear momentum, forex pairs with strong trends, and crypto during bull/bear runs. In ranging markets, MACD crossovers become unreliable - consider using RSI or Stochastic instead when ADX is below 20.

Common Mistakes

  • -Trading every crossover without trend confirmation - check ADX or higher timeframe trend first
  • -Using MACD alone without support/resistance or volume analysis - combine with price action
  • -Ignoring divergence signals - these are the most reliable MACD setups
  • -Using (12,26,9) for all timeframes - consider faster settings for day trading, slower for position trading
  • -Entering at crossovers without waiting for candle close - crossovers can reverse before the bar closes

Frequently Asked Questions

What is the difference between MACD and RSI?

MACD shows trend direction and momentum using moving averages - it tells you if a trend is gaining or losing steam. RSI measures overbought/oversold conditions on a 0-100 scale - it tells you if price is stretched. Use MACD for trend following, RSI for mean reversion.

How to adjust MACD settings for crypto trading?

For volatile crypto markets, consider (10,20,8) for faster, more responsive signals or (15,30,10) for smoother, fewer false signals. Backtest different settings on your specific pair - Bitcoin often works well with (12,26,9) while altcoins may need faster settings due to higher volatility.

Can MACD predict market reversals accurately?

MACD divergence is one of the most reliable reversal signals, especially on daily and weekly charts. In my experience, divergence on higher timeframes (4H+) has a 70-80% success rate when confirmed with price action. However, divergence can sometimes lead to multiple signals before the actual reversal - always wait for confirmation.

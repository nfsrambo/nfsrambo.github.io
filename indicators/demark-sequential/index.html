DeMark Sequential - TD: Formula & Strategy

Learn DeMark Sequential formula, TD setup, TD countdown, exhaustion signals, trend reversal identification, and get code in Python, Rust, Go, and 19 more languages.

Quick Answer

DeMark Sequential counts exhaustion bars. Think of it as a momentum odometer. TD Setup = 9 bars of directional momentum. TD Countdown = 13 more bars signaling exhaustion. When the sequence completes, reversal is likely. It's a timing tool for catching tops and bottoms.

Formula

TD Setup (Uptrend): Close > Close_4 for 9 consecutive bars
TD Setup (Downtrend): Close < Close_4 for 9 consecutive bars
TD Countdown: After Setup, 13 bars with Close < Low_2 (buy) or Close > High_2 (sell)
Completion = Potential Reversal Signal

DeMark Sequential Signal Interpretation

Directional momentum confirmed. Trend may be nearing exhaustion.

TD Countdown completed (13 bars)

Trend exhaustion signal - reversal likely. Time to prepare for turn.

Setup + Countdown in uptrend

Buying exhaustion - potential top. Consider selling or shorting.

Setup + Countdown in downtrend

Selling exhaustion - potential bottom. Consider buying.

How DeMark Sequential Works

DeMark Sequential has two phases. First, TD Setup counts 9 consecutive bars in the same direction (each close higher than the close 4 bars earlier in uptrends, or lower in downtrends). Second, TD Countdown requires 13 bars after Setup with specific conditions. Completion suggests the trend is exhausted and a reversal is likely. The indicator is complex but powerful for identifying major turning points.

DeMark Sequential Trading Strategies

Setup Completion Strategy

Watch for Setup completion (9 bars). Look for reversal at this level. Profit-taking often occurs at Setup completion.

intermediate

Countdown Completion Strategy

Enter when Countdown completes (13 bars). This is the exhaustion signal. Expect reversal soon after Countdown completion.

intermediate

Sequential + RSI Strategy

Use RSI for momentum confirmation. Countdown completion with RSI divergence = strong reversal signal. Higher probability trades.

advanced

Sequential + Price Action Strategy

Wait for reversal candlestick patterns at Setup or Countdown completion. Pin bars or engulfing patterns confirm the signal.

History

DeMark Sequential was developed by Tom DeMark, a renowned technical analyst. DeMark worked with major hedge funds and traders, developing his proprietary indicators. The Sequential system was published in his books and has become widely used by institutional traders.

Best Market Conditions

DeMark Sequential works best in trending markets where clear exhaustion patterns develop. In sideways markets, signals are rare. Use with ADX to confirm trend strength.

Common Mistakes

  • -Using Sequential alone without confirmation
  • -Trading every Setup completion
  • -Ignoring Countdown for confirmation
  • -Using on too short timeframes
  • -Not using price action confirmation

Frequently Asked Questions

What is the best timeframe for DeMark Sequential?

DeMark Sequential works on all timeframes but is most reliable on daily and weekly charts. Shorter timeframes may give more signals but with lower reliability.

How to use DeMark Sequential with Fibonacci?

Use DeMark Sequential for timing and Fibonacci for price levels. Countdown completion at Fibonacci retracement or extension levels gives high probability reversal signals.

What is the difference between Setup and Countdown?

TD Setup identifies directional momentum (9 bars). TD Countdown identifies exhaustion (13 bars after Setup). Setup shows trend strength, Countdown shows when the trend is running out of steam.

Schaff Trend Cycle - STC: Formula & Strategy

Learn Schaff Trend Cycle formula, cycle analysis, momentum measurement, overbought/oversold signals, and get code in Python, Rust, Go, and 19 more languages.

Simulator not available for this indicator

Quick Answer

Schaff Trend Cycle combines cycle analysis with MACD to identify momentum changes. Think of it as MACD optimized for cycle detection. Above 75 = overbought (sell zone). Below 25 = oversold (buy zone). Crossovers give clean entry signals.

Formula

MACD = EMA(Price, Fast) - EMA(Price, Slow)
Cycle = MACD smoothed with cycle length
STC = (Cycle - Lowest(Cycle)) / (Highest(Cycle) - Lowest(Cycle)) × 100
Where:
Fast = 23, Slow = 50 (standard)
Cycle Length = 10 (standard)

STC Signal Interpretation

STC > 75 (Overbought)

Cycle top - overbought. Potential sell or pullback.

STC < 25 (Oversold)

Cycle bottom - oversold. Potential buy or bounce.

STC crosses above 25

Bullish crossover - cycle turning up. Potential buy.

STC crosses below 75

Bearish crossover - cycle turning down. Potential sell.

Divergence with price

Price and STC moving opposite. Cycle divergence - reversal likely.

How Schaff Trend Cycle Works

STC calculates MACD from price, then applies cycle analysis to smooth the MACD line. It normalizes the result to a 0-100 scale. The indicator identifies where price is in its trend cycle. Overbought levels (>75) suggest the cycle is at a top. Oversold levels (<25) suggest the cycle is at a bottom.

Step-by-Step STC Calculation

1

Calculate MACD: EMA(23) - EMA(50)

2

Apply cycle smoothing to MACD

3

Find highest and lowest cycle values

4

Normalize: (Cycle - Low) / (High - Low) × 100

5

0-100 range with 25/75 extremes

6

Watch crossovers and divergences

STC Trading Strategies

Overbought/Oversold Strategy

Buy when STC crosses above 25 (leaving oversold). Sell when STC crosses below 75 (leaving overbought). Cycle-based entries.

beginner

Divergence Strategy

Bullish divergence: price lower low, STC higher low. Bearish divergence: price higher high, STC lower high. High probability signals.

intermediate

STC + Trend Strategy

Use ADX for trend confirmation. Trade STC signals when ADX > 25. Avoid signals in weak trends.

intermediate

STC + RSI Strategy

Use STC for cycle timing and RSI for momentum. STC oversold + RSI oversold = strong buy. Both overbought = strong sell.

advanced

History

Schaff Trend Cycle was developed by Doug Schaff in the 1990s. Schaff created it to combine MACD's momentum measurement with cycle analysis. The indicator has gained popularity among traders who use cycle analysis for timing.

Best Market Conditions

STC works best in trending markets with clear cycles. In sideways markets, it can give false signals. Use with ADX for trend confirmation.

Common Mistakes

  • -Using STC alone without confirmation
  • -Trading every overbought/oversold signal
  • -Ignoring divergence signals
  • -Using default settings without testing
  • -Using STC in sideways markets

Frequently Asked Questions

What is the best STC setting for crypto?

For crypto, standard (10,23,50) works for swing trading. For day trading, use (10,15,30). For Bitcoin, standard settings. Altcoins may need (10,20,40).

How to use STC with MACD?

STC is based on MACD. Use MACD for momentum and STC for cycle timing. When both align, signals are much stronger.

What is the difference between STC and MACD?

STC adds cycle analysis to MACD and normalizes the result. MACD shows momentum direction. STC shows where price is in the cycle.

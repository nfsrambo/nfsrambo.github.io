Accumulation Distribution - A/D Line: Formula & Strategy

Quick Answer

Accumulation Distribution Line shows whether money is flowing into or out of a stock. Think of it as a volume-powered trend confirmation tool. Rising A/D = smart money buying (accumulation). Falling A/D = smart money selling (distribution). Divergence from price is the most powerful signal.

Formula

Money Flow Multiplier = ((Close - Low) - (High - Close)) / (High - Low)
Money Flow Volume = Money Flow Multiplier × Volume
Accumulation Distribution = Cumulative sum of Money Flow Volume

A/D Line Signal Interpretation

A/D Line rising

Accumulation happening - money flowing in. Bullish.

A/D Line falling

Distribution happening - money flowing out. Bearish.

A/D Line makes higher highs

Strong buying pressure. Price likely to follow higher.

A/D Line makes lower lows

Strong selling pressure. Price likely to follow lower.

Divergence with price

Price and A/D moving opposite. Reversal likely ahead.

How Accumulation Distribution Works

A/D Line calculates money flow based on the close's position within the daily range. When close is in the upper part of the range, money flows in (accumulation). When close is in the lower part, money flows out (distribution). The cumulative line shows whether buying or selling pressure is dominant over time. A/D often leads price, making it a valuable leading indicator.

Step-by-Step Accumulation Distribution Calculation

1

Calculate Money Flow Multiplier: ((Close-Low) - (High-Close)) / (High-Low)

2

Multiply by Volume to get Money Flow Volume

3

Add Money Flow Volume to previous A/D value

4

Positive values = accumulation, negative = distribution

5

Plot cumulative line

6

Watch for divergence between A/D and price

A/D Line Trading Strategies

A/D Trend Confirmation

Price rising with A/D rising = strong uptrend. Price rising with A/D falling = weak uptrend. Use for trend confirmation.

beginner

A/D Divergence Strategy

Bullish divergence: price lower low, A/D higher low = buy. Bearish divergence: price higher high, A/D lower high = sell.

intermediate

A/D + Volume Strategy

A/D rising with volume increasing = strong accumulation. A/D falling with volume increasing = strong distribution.

intermediate

A/D + Support/Resistance Strategy

Watch A/D at support/resistance levels. A/D divergence at key levels gives stronger reversal signals.

Pros & Cons

Advantages

  • -Shows actual money flow
  • -Divergence signals are highly reliable
  • -Often leads price movements
  • -Works across all markets
  • -Simple to use and interpret

Limitations

  • -Cumulative - can drift over time
  • -Less effective in low volume markets
  • -Requires volume data
  • -Can show divergence multiple times
  • -Best used with other indicators

History

Accumulation Distribution was developed by Marc Chaikin as an improvement over OBV. Chaikin's innovation was using the close's position within the range to determine money flow direction. The A/D Line is a core component of Chaikin's trading system.

Best Market Conditions

A/D Line works best in liquid markets with consistent volume. It's particularly effective in stocks and crypto. Use with ADX for trend confirmation.

Common Mistakes

  • -Using A/D alone without price confirmation
  • -Ignoring divergence - the most reliable signal
  • -Using A/D on low volume markets
  • -Expecting exact price targets from A/D
  • -Using A/D without volume data

Frequently Asked Questions

How to use A/D with MACD?

Use MACD for momentum and A/D for volume confirmation. When both bullish, trend is strong. A/D divergence with MACD gives high probability reversal signals.

What is the difference between A/D and Chaikin Money Flow?

A/D is cumulative (running total). CMF is smoothed over periods. A/D shows long-term accumulation, CMF shows shorter-term money flow.

Does A/D work in crypto?

Yes, A/D works in crypto markets with reliable volume data. A/D divergence is effective for catching Bitcoin and altcoin reversals.

DPO - Detrended Price Oscillator: Formula & Strategy

Learn DPO formula, cycle identification, price analysis, trend removal, and get code in Python, Rust, Go, and 19 more languages.

Simulator not available for this indicator

Quick Answer

DPO removes trend to show price cycles. Think of it as price with the noise of long-term trends stripped away. Above zero = price is above its cycle average (bullish). Below zero = price is below its cycle average (bearish). DPO helps identify cyclical overbought/oversold conditions.

Formula

DPO = Price - SMA(Price, N/2 + 1)
Where:
Price = Current closing price
N = Period (standard 20)
SMA = Simple Moving Average
DPO is shifted forward by N/2 + 1 periods to align with price

DPO Signal Interpretation

DPO above 0

Price above detrended average - bullish cycle phase.

DPO below 0

Price below detrended average - bearish cycle phase.

DPO crossing above 0

Bullish cycle start - price moving above average. Potential buy.

DPO crossing below 0

Bearish cycle start - price moving below average. Potential sell.

DPO at cycle extremes

Overbought or oversold within cycle. Potential reversal.

How DPO Works

DPO removes the long-term trend component from price, leaving only the cyclical component. By subtracting a shifted SMA from current price, DPO reveals when price is above or below its trend-adjusted average. This helps identify cyclical patterns and overbought/oversold conditions that are hidden by the trend. The indicator is shifted forward to align with price action.

Step-by-Step DPO Calculation

1

Calculate SMA of closing prices over N periods

2

Shift SMA forward by N/2 + 1 periods

3

Subtract shifted SMA from current price

4

Positive = bullish cycle, negative = bearish cycle

5

Watch for zero line crossovers for cycle changes

6

Use extremes for cycle timing

DPO Trading Strategies

Zero Line Crossover Strategy

Buy when DPO crosses above zero (cycle turns bullish). Sell when DPO crosses below zero (cycle turns bearish).

beginner

Cycle Extreme Strategy

Buy when DPO reaches extreme lows (oversold). Sell when DPO reaches extreme highs (overbought). Mean reversion within cycle.

intermediate

DPO + Trend Strategy

Use EMA for trend direction and DPO for cycle timing. Enter when DPO turns positive in uptrend. Exit when DPO turns negative.

intermediate

DPO Divergence Strategy

Bullish divergence: price lower low, DPO higher low. Bearish divergence: price higher high, DPO lower high. Cycle divergence signals reversals.

advanced

Pros & Cons

Advantages

  • -Reveals hidden cyclical patterns
  • -Removes trend noise
  • -Clear zero line signals
  • -Works across all markets
  • -Useful for cycle analysis

Limitations

  • -Lagging indicator - shifted forward
  • -Period selection affects cycles shown
  • -Less widely known
  • -Can be noisy in choppy markets
  • -Best used with other indicators

History

DPO was developed to help traders identify price cycles by removing the trend component. The concept of detrending prices has been used in technical analysis for decades. DPO gained popularity as cycle analysis became more common in trading.

Best Market Conditions

DPO works best in markets with clear price cycles. In strongly trending markets, DPO may stay on one side of zero for extended periods. Use with trend indicators to distinguish between cyclical moves and trend moves.

Common Mistakes

  • -Using DPO alone without trend context
  • -Expecting cycles to repeat perfectly
  • -Using wrong period for timeframe
  • -Trading every zero line crossover without confirmation
  • -Ignoring divergence signals

Frequently Asked Questions

What is the best DPO period for crypto?

For crypto, 20-period DPO works for swing trading. For day trading, use 10-period. For Bitcoin, 20-period is standard. Altcoins with faster cycles may need 14-period.

How to use DPO with MACD?

Use MACD for momentum and DPO for cycle timing. When both bullish, cycle and momentum align. DPO divergence with MACD gives strong reversal signals.

What is the shift in DPO formula?

DPO is shifted forward by N/2 + 1 periods. This aligns the oscillator with price action, making it easier to compare DPO signals with current price movements.

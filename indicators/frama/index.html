FRAMA - Fractal Adaptive MA: Formula & Strategy

Quick Answer

FRAMA uses fractal geometry to adapt to market conditions. Think of it as a moving average that uses math to detect market structure. In trending markets, it follows price closely. In sideways markets, it smooths noise. Price above FRAMA = uptrend. Below = downtrend.

Formula

N1 = Highest High - Lowest Low over first half of period
N2 = Highest High - Lowest Low over second half of period
N3 = Highest High - Lowest Low over full period
Fractal Dimension (D) = (log(N1 + N2) - log(N3)) / log(2)
Alpha = exp(-4.6 × (D - 1))
FRAMA = FRAMA_prev + Alpha × (Close - FRAMA_prev)

How FRAMA Works

FRAMA uses fractal dimension analysis to measure market structure. It compares price ranges across different time periods within the lookback window. When the market is trending (low fractal dimension), FRAMA follows price closely. When the market is ranging (high fractal dimension), FRAMA smooths noise. This fractal-based approach provides superior adaptation to market conditions.

Pros & Cons

Advantages

  • -Follows trends closely in trending markets
  • -Smooths noise effectively in sideways markets
  • -Less lag than fixed moving averages
  • -Works across all timeframes and markets

Limitations

  • -Requires understanding of fractal dimension
  • -Less widely known than traditional MAs
  • -Computational overhead for real-time trading

History

FRAMA was developed by John Ehlers, a pioneer in technical analysis, in his 2001 book 'Cybernetic Analysis for Stocks and Futures'. Ehlers applied fractal geometry to create an adaptive moving average that responds to market structure. The indicator uses the fractal dimension to determine market noise levels.

Best Market Conditions

FRAMA performs well in all market conditions due to its fractal-based adaptation. In trending markets, it follows price closely. In ranging markets, it smooths noise. This makes FRAMA valuable for systematic trading across various market regimes.

Common Mistakes

  • -Using FRAMA alone without confirmation
  • -Choosing wrong period for timeframe
  • -Trading every crossover without momentum confirmation
  • -Expecting zero lag - FRAMA reduces but doesn't eliminate lag
  • -Using default settings without testing on specific asset

Frequently Asked Questions

What is the best FRAMA period for crypto?

For crypto, try (16,4) for day trading and (20,4) for swing trading. The fractal-based adaptation works across different market conditions. Backtest on your specific crypto pair.

How to use FRAMA with MACD?

Use FRAMA for trend direction and MACD for momentum confirmation. When both agree, trend is strong. When they diverge, trend may be weakening - potential reversal.

What is Fractal Dimension?

Fractal Dimension measures market noise or inefficiency. It ranges from 1.0 (perfect trend) to 2.0 (perfect random walk). FRAMA uses this to determine its speed - trending markets = fast, ranging = slow.

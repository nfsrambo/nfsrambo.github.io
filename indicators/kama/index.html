KAMA - Kaufman's Adaptive MA: Formula & Strategy

Learn KAMA formula, adaptive smoothing, noise filtering, trend identification, and get code in Python, Rust, Go, and 19 more languages.

Quick Answer

KAMA adapts to market conditions automatically. In strong trends, it follows price closely. In sideways markets, it smooths out noise. Think of it as a smart moving average that knows when to be fast and when to be slow. Price above KAMA = uptrend. Below = downtrend.

Formula

Efficiency Ratio (ER) = |Change over N periods| / Sum of daily moves over N periods
Smoothing Constant (SC) = (ER × (Fastest SC - Slowest SC) + Slowest SC)²
KAMA = KAMA_prev + SC × (Close - KAMA_prev)
Where:
N = Period (typically 10)
Fastest SC = 2/(Fast EMA + 1)
Slowest SC = 2/(Slow EMA + 1)

KAMA Signal Interpretation

Price above KAMA

Bullish trend - KAMA is following the uptrend closely. Strong momentum.

Price below KAMA

Bearish trend - KAMA is following the downtrend closely. Strong momentum.

Price crosses above KAMA

Bullish crossover - trend turning up. KAMA adapts to new trend.

Price crosses below KAMA

Bearish crossover - trend turning down. KAMA adapts to new trend.

KAMA flattens

Market is ranging - KAMA smoothing noise. Avoid trend strategies.

How KAMA Works

KAMA uses the Efficiency Ratio to measure market noise. In strong trends, ER is high - KAMA follows price closely (fast). In sideways markets, ER is low - KAMA smooths noise (slow). This adaptive behavior makes KAMA more effective than fixed-sensitivity moving averages across different market conditions.

KAMA Trading Strategies

KAMA Crossover Strategy

Buy when price crosses above KAMA. Sell when price crosses below KAMA. KAMA's adaptive nature reduces whipsaws in sideways markets.

Pros & Cons

  • -Follows trends closely in strong moves
  • -Less lag than fixed moving averages
  • -Works across all timeframes and markets

  • -Less widely known than traditional MAs
  • -May not perform well in extremely volatile markets

History

KAMA was developed by Perry Kaufman in his 1998 book 'Trading Systems and Methods'. Kaufman, a renowned trading system developer, created KAMA to address the problem of fixed-speed moving averages. His innovation was using market noise to determine the optimal speed, making KAMA truly adaptive.

Best Market Conditions

KAMA performs well in all market conditions due to its adaptive nature. In trending markets, it follows price closely. In ranging markets, it smooths noise. This makes KAMA valuable for trend-following systems across various market regimes.

Common Mistakes

  • -Using KAMA alone without confirmation
  • -Choosing wrong ER period for timeframe
  • -Trading every crossover without momentum confirmation
  • -Expecting zero lag - KAMA reduces but doesn't eliminate lag
  • -Using default settings without testing on specific asset

Frequently Asked Questions

What is the best KAMA setting for crypto?

For crypto, try (10,2,30) for day trading and (10,2,50) for swing trading. The adaptive nature means it works across different market conditions. Backtest on your specific crypto pair.

How to use KAMA with RSI?

Use KAMA for trend direction and RSI for momentum. When KAMA shows uptrend and RSI oversold, it's a strong buy. When KAMA shows downtrend and RSI overbought, it's a strong sell.

What is the Efficiency Ratio?

Efficiency Ratio measures market noise - ratio of direction to total movement. High ER (0.8+) = strong trend. Low ER (0.3-) = sideways market. KAMA uses ER to determine its speed.

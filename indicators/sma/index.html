SMA - Simple Moving Average: Formula & Strategy

Quick Answer

SMA is the average price over a set period. Think of it as a trend line that smooths out price noise. When price is above SMA, trend is up. Below SMA, trend is down. SMA crossovers with other periods give trading signals.

Formula

SMA = (Sum of Close Prices over N periods) / N
Where N = Number of periods
Example: 50-day SMA = (Sum of last 50 closes) / 50

SMA Signal Interpretation

Price above SMA

Bullish trend - price is above average. Look for buy opportunities.

Price below SMA

Bearish trend - price is below average. Look for sell opportunities.

Price crosses above SMA

Bullish crossover - trend may be turning up. Potential buy signal.

Price crosses below SMA

Bearish crossover - trend may be turning down. Potential sell signal.

SMA acts as support/resistance

Price often bounces off SMA in trends. Watch for rejection at SMA levels.

How SMA Works

SMA calculates the average closing price over a set number of periods. Each price point gets equal weight. As new prices arrive, the oldest price drops out. This creates a smooth line that represents the average price over time. SMA helps identify trend direction, potential support/resistance levels, and crossover signals.

Step-by-Step SMA Calculation

1

Choose your period (e.g., 20 days, 50 days, 200 days)

2

Sum all closing prices for that period

3

Divide by the number of periods

4

When new data arrives, drop the oldest and add the newest

5

Recalculate for each new period

SMA Trading Strategies

Price Crossover Strategy

Buy when price crosses above SMA. Sell when price crosses below SMA. Simple and effective for trending markets.

beginner

Multiple SMA Strategy

Use two SMAs (e.g., 50 and 200). Buy when 50 crosses above 200 (Golden Cross). Sell when 50 crosses below 200 (Death Cross). Major trend reversal signals.

intermediate

SMA Bounce Strategy

In uptrends, buy when price pulls back to SMA and bounces. In downtrends, sell when price rallies to SMA and fails. SMA acts as dynamic support/resistance.

intermediate

SMA Confluence Strategy

When multiple SMAs (20, 50, 200) align in the same direction, trend strength increases. Enter when price confirms the alignment with momentum indicators.

advanced

Pros & Cons

Advantages

  • -Simple to calculate - just average price over periods
  • -Smooths out noise - shows clear trend direction
  • -Acts as dynamic support and resistance levels
  • -Works across all timeframes and markets
  • -Crossovers provide clear entry and exit signals

Limitations

  • -Lagging indicator - signals after price has moved
  • -Equal weight to all prices - treats old data same as new
  • -Can get whipsawed in sideways markets
  • -Slow to react to sudden price changes

History

SMA is one of the oldest and most basic technical indicators. Its roots go back to early market analysis when traders manually calculated averages to identify trends. Despite its simplicity, SMA remains a core tool for modern traders and forms the foundation for many other indicators like MACD and Bollinger Bands.

Best Market Conditions

SMA works best in trending markets where price moves in one direction. In sideways markets, SMA crossovers give false signals. Combine with ADX to confirm trend strength - use SMA signals when ADX > 25.

Common Mistakes

  • -Using SMA alone without trend confirmation
  • -Using same SMA period for all timeframes
  • -Trading every crossover without volume or momentum confirmation
  • -Ignoring SMA as support/resistance in trends
  • -Using SMA on very short timeframes where noise dominates

Frequently Asked Questions

What is the best SMA period for intraday trading?

For intraday, 9, 20, and 50-period SMAs are popular. The 20-period SMA is most common for 5-minute and 15-minute charts. Test different periods on your specific instrument to find what works best.

How to use SMA with MACD?

Use SMA for trend direction and MACD for momentum confirmation. When price is above SMA (uptrend) and MACD shows bullish crossover, the signal is stronger. When they conflict, wait for alignment.

What is the Golden Cross and Death Cross?

Golden Cross occurs when 50-day SMA crosses above 200-day SMA - a major bullish signal. Death Cross occurs when 50-day SMA crosses below 200-day SMA - a major bearish signal. These are watched by institutional traders.

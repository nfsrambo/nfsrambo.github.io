Quick Answer

How Absorption Works

Absorption occurs when institutions trade large volume without moving price. They absorb all available orders at a level, preventing price movement while building positions. At support, buying absorption indicates accumulation before markup. At resistance, selling absorption indicates distribution before markdown. The pattern reveals institutional intent before price confirms. Multiple absorption bars within short period indicate strong conviction and pending directional move.

Absorption Trading Strategies

Breakout Anticipation

Enter when absorption completes. Place stop below absorption level (support) or above (resistance). Target next structural level.

Absorption Scalping

Trade immediate reaction after absorption. Quick 1-2 ATR target. Tight stops. Works well in liquid markets.

Multiple Absorption Swing

Wait for 2-3 absorption bars at same level. Larger position. Hold for major move. Stop beyond the absorption cluster.

Absorption with Divergence

Combine absorption with delta divergence. Highest probability setup. Full position size. Confident target.

Absorption Strength Assessment

Volume Magnitude

2-3x average = moderate absorption. 4-5x+ = extreme absorption. Higher multiple = stronger signal.

Price Contraction

Tighter range indicates stronger absorption. 0.2-0.3% range very strong. 0.5-1% moderate.

Level Importance

Absorption stronger at major S/R levels. Multiple tests of same level increase significance.

Concept Origins

Absorption patterns have been recognized by floor traders and pit traders for decades. Electronic trading and footprint charts made the pattern visible to retail traders. The concept relates to Wyckoff's accumulation/distribution theory and auction market theory.

Best Conditions for Absorption

Most reliable in: high-volume liquid markets (ES, NQ, major forex, large-cap stocks), defined trading ranges with clear S/R, active institutional participation. Less reliable in: low-volume stocks, crypto during off-hours, news-driven moves, extremely low volatility environments.

Common Absorption Trading Errors

Biggest mistakes: trading absorption without S/R context (just high volume bars), entering before absorption completes (premature), ignoring broader trend (counter-trend absorption has lower success), using too tight stops (absorption zones may retest), and not waiting for multiple confirmations.

Frequently Asked Questions

How do I distinguish absorption from low-activity periods?

Absorption requires HIGH volume with small range. Low activity has low volume and small range. Compare volume to 20-period average - absorption requires 2x+ normal volume.

Can absorption occur without immediate breakout?

Yes, price may consolidate at absorption level for additional bars or re-test before breaking out. This is normal - institutions continue absorbing. Maintain position or add on re-test.

What data feed needed for absorption detection?

Need tick data with bid/ask to calculate delta. Many platforms provide footprint charts that show absorption. TradingView, Sierra Chart, ATAS, and QuantConnect support absorption detection.

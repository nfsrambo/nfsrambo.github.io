CIR Model: Cox-Ingersoll-Ross Interest Rates

Master Cox-Ingersoll-Ross interest rate modeling with mean reversion and non-negative rates. Learn bond pricing and yield curve valuation.

CIR Formula

Short rate dynamics:
dr_t = κ(θ - r_t)dt + σ√r_t dW_t

Where:
r_t = Short rate at time t
κ = Speed of mean reversion
θ = Long-term mean level
σ = Interest rate volatility
√r_t = Square root prevents negative rates

Positivity condition:
2κθ > σ²

Bond price:
P(t,T) = A(t,T)·exp(-B(t,T)·r_t)

Where A and B involve non-central chi-squared
parameters: a = 2κθ/σ², b = 2κ/(σ²(1-e^{-κ(T-t)}))

How CIR Works

The CIR model extends Vasicek by making volatility proportional to √r_t. This ensures rates remain positive: when r_t approaches zero, volatility approaches zero. The Feller condition 2κθ > σ² guarantees the origin is unattainable. This produces more realistic interest rate dynamics with positive rates.

Financial Applications

Bond Pricing

Price zero-coupon and coupon bonds with CIR formulas. More realistic than Vasicek for low rate environments.

Bond Options

Price European options on bonds. CIR provides analytic formulas (non-central chi-squared based).

Yield Curve Modeling

Generate realistic yield curves with positive rates. Essential for fixed income valuation.

Risk Management

Model positive rate dynamics. Important for insurers and pension funds with long-term liabilities.

Parameter Estimation Guide

MLE Estimation

Use non-central chi-squared transition density. More complex than Vasicek but provides efficient estimates.

GMM Estimation

Method of moments using conditional moments. Simpler than MLE for practitioners.

Market Calibration

Fit model to bond prices. Use nonlinear least squares. Ensure Feller condition satisfied.

The CIR Contribution

Cox, Ingersoll, and Ross (1985) developed their model as a general equilibrium model. The square root diffusion was chosen to ensure positive rates while maintaining tractability. CIR remains one of the most widely used short rate models.

Implementation Tips

Use non-central chi-squared distribution for exact simulation. For Euler discretization, use √max(r_t,0) to avoid negative rates. Check Feller condition strictly.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is the Feller condition in CIR?

2κθ > σ² ensures rates remain positive and cannot reach zero. It's a condition on parameters that guarantees the boundary is unattainable.

Can CIR handle negative interest rates?

No, CIR explicitly prevents negative rates. This is a feature for normal environments but a limitation when rates are negative (as in recent years).

How accurate is CIR for long-term bonds?

CIR works reasonably for short-term bonds but may misprice long-term bonds. Term structure extensions (Hull-White) improve long-end accuracy.

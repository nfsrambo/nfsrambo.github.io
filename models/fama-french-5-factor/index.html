Fama-French 5-Factor Model: Complete Asset Pricing

Fama-French 5-Factor Formula

Rᵢ - R_f = α + β_M·(R_m - R_f) + β_SMB·SMB + β_HML·HML + β_RMW·RMW + β_CMA·CMA + ε

Factors:
SMB = Small Minus Big (size)
HML = High Minus Low (value)
RMW = Robust Minus Weak (profitability)
CMA = Conservative Minus Aggressive (investment)

Profitability = Revenues - COGS - SG&A / Book Equity
Investment = Change in Total Assets / Total Assets

How Fama-French 5-Factor Works

The 5-factor model extends the 3-factor by adding profitability (RMW) and investment (CMA). RMW captures the higher returns of profitable firms. CMA captures the higher returns of firms that invest conservatively. These factors address limitations of the 3-factor model, particularly for portfolios sorted on profitability and investment.

Factor Construction Guide

Profitability Definition

Use operating profitability: (Revenue - COGS - SG&A) / Book Equity. Higher profitability indicates higher expected returns.

Investment Definition

Use growth in total assets: (Assets_t - Assets_t-1) / Assets_t-1. Lower investment (conservative) indicates higher expected returns.

RMW Construction

Average return of high profitability minus low profitability portfolios. 2×3 sorts using size and profitability breakpoints.

The 5-Factor Evolution

Fama and French (2015) added profitability and investment factors after recognizing 3-factor model's limitations. The 5-factor model better explains anomalies but makes HML redundant. The model continues to evolve with ongoing research.

Implementation Tips

Use Kenneth French's 5-factor data. Check for multicollinearity (HML often redundant). Consider 60-month rolling regressions. Test for factor stability over subperiods.

Frequently Asked Questions

Why did Fama-French add RMW and CMA?

The 3-factor model couldn't explain returns sorted on profitability and investment. RMW and CMA capture these return patterns, improving explanatory power significantly.

Does 5-factor model replace 3-factor?

For most purposes, yes. 5-factor provides better explanatory power. However, 3-factor remains valuable for its simplicity and for portfolios where HML remains significant.

What about momentum in 5-factor?

5-factor still doesn't explain momentum. Carhart 4-factor adds momentum. Momentum appears orthogonal to the five factors.

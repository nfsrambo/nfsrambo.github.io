CAPM: Capital Asset Pricing Model Guide

Master the Capital Asset Pricing Model for expected return calculation. Learn beta estimation, security market line, and portfolio analysis with CAPM.

Quick Answer

CAPM: E(Rᵢ) = R_f + βᵢ·(R_m - R_f). Expected return equals risk-free rate plus beta times market risk premium. Beta measures systematic risk relative to market.

CAPM Formula

E(Rᵢ) = R_f + βᵢ·[E(R_m) - R_f]

Where:
E(Rᵢ) = Expected return on asset i
R_f = Risk-free rate
βᵢ = Beta of asset i
E(R_m) = Expected return on market portfolio
[E(R_m) - R_f] = Market risk premium

Beta calculation:
βᵢ = Cov(Rᵢ, R_m) / Var(R_m)

Security Market Line (SML):
E(Rᵢ) = R_f + (E(R_m)-R_f)·βᵢ

CAPM Diagnostics

β = 1.0

Same risk as market (average stock)

β > 1.0

More volatile than market (cyclical/tech)

β < 1.0

Less volatile than market (defensive/utility)

β = 0

No systematic risk (risk-free asset)

Key Assumptions

  • •Investors are risk-averse and mean-variance optimizers
  • •Perfect capital markets (no taxes, transaction costs)
  • •All investors have homogeneous expectations
  • •Unlimited borrowing/lending at risk-free rate
  • •Single-period investment horizon

Limitations

  • •Single-factor model (only market risk)
  • •Assumes linear risk-return relationship
  • •Empirical anomalies (size, value effects)
  • •Beta stability questionable over time
  • •Relies on market portfolio (unobservable)

How CAPM Works

CAPM establishes a linear relationship between expected return and systematic risk (beta). The model assumes investors are compensated for two components: the time value of money (risk-free rate) and the systematic risk premium (beta × market risk premium). Beta measures how much an asset's returns move with the market. This forms the Security Market Line (SML), which represents the equilibrium relationship between risk and return.

Financial Applications

Cost of Equity

CAPM is the standard method for calculating cost of equity in WACC. Used for capital budgeting and project evaluation.

Performance Evaluation

Calculate alpha (Jensen's Alpha) = Actual return - CAPM expected return. Positive alpha indicates outperformance.

Portfolio Construction

Use beta to construct portfolios with desired risk profiles. Combine low-beta defensive and high-beta aggressive stocks.

Asset Pricing

Determine fair expected returns for assets. Compare market prices to CAPM-implied values for valuation.

Pros and Cons

Advantages

  • -Simple and intuitive
  • -One-factor model (easy to estimate)
  • -Established theoretical foundation
  • -Widely accepted in practice
  • -Regulatory acceptance (utility ratemaking)

Limitations

  • -Single factor insufficient
  • -Relies on unobservable market portfolio
  • -Empirical anomalies exist
  • -Beta instability over time
  • -Assumes linear risk-return tradeoff

Beta Estimation Guide

OLS Regression

Regress asset excess returns on market excess returns. Use 3-5 years of monthly data or 1-2 years of daily data.

Beta Adjustment

Blume (1975) adjusted beta: β_adj = 0.67·β_est + 0.33·1.0. Corrects for mean reversion in beta.

Rolling Beta

Estimate beta over rolling windows (60 months). Track beta stability. Useful for dynamic portfolio management.

The Nobel Prize Model

William Sharpe (1964), John Lintner (1965), and Jan Mossin (1966) independently developed CAPM. Sharpe shared the 1990 Nobel Prize with Harry Markowitz and Merton Miller. CAPM became the foundation of modern finance, despite decades of empirical challenges.

Derivation from Markowitz

CAPM derived from Markowitz's mean-variance optimization. With homogeneous beliefs and ability to borrow/lend at R_f, the efficient frontier becomes linear (Capital Market Line). The tangency portfolio is the market portfolio. Each asset's expected return follows SML: E(Rᵢ) = R_f + βᵢ·(E(R_m)-R_f).

Frequently Asked Questions

What is a good beta for a stock?

Depends on industry. Utilities: 0.5-0.8. Consumer staples: 0.7-0.9. S&P 500: 1.0. Technology: 1.2-1.8. High beta means higher risk and higher expected return.

How often does beta change?

Beta estimates change over time. Use rolling 36-60 month windows to track stability. Significant changes occur with business model shifts or market environment changes.

Can CAPM predict stock returns?

CAPM explains cross-sectional variation partly. R² typically 20-40% for individual stocks. Multi-factor models (Fama-French) improve predictive power significantly.

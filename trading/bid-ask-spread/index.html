How Bid-Ask Spread Works

The bid-ask spread represents the difference between the highest price a buyer is willing to pay (bid) and the lowest price a seller is willing to accept (ask). Market makers earn this spread by providing liquidity. Traders pay the spread when using market orders (crossing the spread). Limit orders avoid paying the spread but risk not being filled. Spread width is the primary measure of market liquidity: narrow spreads = high liquidity, wide spreads = low liquidity.

Spread Applications

Transaction Cost Analysis

Track effective spread vs quoted spread. Measure slippage and market impact. Optimize order routing based on spread conditions.

Liquidity Timing

Trade during peak liquidity (spreads narrow). Forex: London-New York overlap. Stocks: regular trading hours. Avoid lunch and overnight.

Market Making

Capture spread by placing limit orders. Profit when orders cross. Requires inventory management and adverse selection protection.

Arbitrage Detection

Wide spreads indicate arbitrage opportunities. Cross-exchange spreads in crypto. Futures vs spot arbitrage when spreads diverge.

Pros and Cons

Advantages

  • -Direct measure of liquidity
  • -Easy to calculate and monitor
  • -Predictive of transaction costs
  • -Works across all asset classes
  • -Real-time availability

Limitations

  • -Doesn't measure depth beyond best level
  • -Quoted spread ≠ effective spread
  • -Can be manipulated (spoofing)
  • -Less informative for large orders
  • -Varies significantly by time of day

Spread Risk Management

Spread Cost Budgeting

Allocate 0.5-1% of expected returns to spread costs. Track realized vs expected spread. Adjust position sizes when spreads widen.

Limit Order Placement

Place limit orders inside spread when possible. Improve fill probability while reducing cost. Use order book depth for placement.

Spread Contingency

Widening spreads trigger position reduction. Stop trading if spread > 3× average. Resume when spreads normalize.

Spread Patterns by Asset

Forex majors: 0.1-1 pip during peak hours, widening to 2-5 pips overnight. Stocks: $0.01 for liquid names, 1-5% for small caps. Crypto: 0.01-0.1% on majors, 0.5-2% on altcoins. Futures: 0.25-1 tick depending on contract.

Common Spread Mistakes

Top mistakes: ignoring spread cost in backtests, using market orders during wide spreads, trading during known low-liquidity periods, not accounting for slippage beyond spread, and failing to adapt order type to spread conditions.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is a normal bid-ask spread for stocks?

High-volume stocks (AAPL, MSFT): $0.01 spread. Medium volume: $0.02-0.05. Low volume penny stocks: $0.10-0.50 or 5-10%. Always check specific stock liquidity before trading.

How does spread affect day trading profitability?

For scalpers, spread cost can consume 20-50% of gross profits. Target 2:1 risk-reward minimum. Use limit orders to reduce cost. Trade only during peak liquidity hours.

Can I profit from bid-ask spread?

Yes, through market making. Place limit orders on both sides of book. Capture spread when orders fill and cross. Requires speed, inventory management, and adverse selection protection.
